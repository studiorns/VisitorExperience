Abu Dhabi Visitor Journey Insights

Department of Culture and Tourism - Abu Dhabi

Qualitative Research Findings & Strategic Recommendations

Executive Summary

This report presents key insights from comprehensive qualitative research conducted across 24 focus groups spanning six key markets (India, Russia, China, USA, Europe, GCC). The research explored the complete visitor journey to Abu Dhabi, identifying pain points, moments of delight, and opportunities for enhancement.

The findings reveal that while Abu Dhabi delivers strong experiences across most journey touchpoints, there are significant opportunities to strengthen emotional connections, enhance digital discovery, improve pre-arrival engagement, and activate post-visit advocacy.

This document synthesizes the research findings into actionable insights and strategic recommendations to elevate Abu Dhabi's visitor experience and strengthen its position as a premier global destination.

Research Methodology

The qualitative research phase involved 24 focus groups across six key markets (India, Russia, China, USA, Europe, GCC), with all participants being tourists who had visited Abu Dhabi. The fieldwork was conducted in Q1 2025, from February 6th to March 10th. Each focus group included three homogeneous participants from the same country/region to extract distinct insights about their Abu Dhabi journey.

All participants were decision-makers for travel and destination planning, responsible and/or involved in the trip planning process. The sample included a mix of single/couple travelers and family/group travelers, all visiting for leisure purposes. This diverse sample allowed us to capture a comprehensive range of visitor experiences and perspectives.

Travel Behavior Performance Ratings

The research identified how well Abu Dhabi currently meets different visitor needs and travel behaviors:

  • Personal Growth via Genuine Experiences: LOW - Though seen to embody cultural mix, many visitors do not identify personal growth as a trigger to visit. Deeper interactions with local culture would enhance learning and knowledge.
  • Escape and Adventure: HIGH - Abu Dhabi performs well as a high-end, welcoming, safe and relaxing escape with premium offerings for adrenaline escapes, blending land and sea-based activities.
  • Balanced Scheduling and Flexibility: HIGH - The destination caters effectively to planning itineraries that balance exploration and rest, helping visitors benefit from a mixed tempo of activities.
  • Discovery and Novelty: MEDIUM - The allure of discovering the capital city of the UAE triggers curiosity, but there's opportunity to create a greater emotional connection to the city itself.

Journey Pillar Performance Summary

Pillar 1: Inspiration & Decision Making

Performance: MEDIUM

Abu Dhabi performs strongly in sparking interest through visual content and word-of-mouth, but falls short in converting inspiration into confident decision-making. Visitors face gaps in clarity, consistency, and trust when trying to move forward with planning or booking. Poor visibility of official content on search engines and social platforms, perception skewed toward luxury, and lack of culturally sensitive planning content are key pain points.

Key Market Nuances: Chinese visitors face language barriers, Indians are price-sensitive, Russians lack culturally contextual content, Americans have safety concerns, Europeans struggle to find official sources, and GCC visitors seek cultural fit assurance.

Pillar 2: Operations & Logistics

Performance: MEDIUM

Direct flight connectivity, hassle-free visa entry, accommodation choice, and currency stability are key considerations. Limited direct flights from USA and China, accommodation lacking cultural personalization, unfamiliar payment options, and poorly communicated visa details are major pain points.

Key Market Nuances: Chinese visitors need digital payment integration, Indians focus on visa-free entry, Russians need payment workarounds, Americans want direct flights, Europeans seek diverse accommodations, and GCC visitors prefer private serviced accommodations.

Pillar 3: Experiences & Activities Booking

Performance: MEDIUM

Visitors value choice of world-class activities, advance booking options with discounts, and good online offerings. Limited or incorrect information, technical glitches during booking, non-existent deals on social media, and encountering "fine print" at the wrong time are key pain points.

Key Market Nuances: Chinese visitors want comprehensive city tours, Indians seek early bird discounts, Russians encounter website glitches, Americans want curated suggestions, Europeans find outdated information, and GCC visitors prefer phone/WhatsApp customer service.

Pillar 4: Waiting Time & Excitement

Performance: MEDIUM

After booking, visitors feel excited but largely self-manage plans, seeking clarity, control, and personalized guidance. Lack of official communication post-booking, uncertainty around trip structuring, and fragmented information across platforms are key pain points.

Key Market Nuances: Chinese visitors expect app-based updates, Indians want ready-to-use itineraries, Russians need pre-arrival support, Americans seek reassurance emails, Europeans want clear trip expectations, and GCC visitors expect warm welcome messages.

Pillar 5: Actual Experiences

Performance: MEDIUM

Visitors want to feel they made the right decision from airport arrival to departure. Smooth airport experience, exceptional service, easily available currency exchange, fast visa processes, and reliable transfers are key delight factors. Transport challenges, currency exchange issues, rushed tours, expensive beverages, and limited cuisine options are pain points.

Key Market Nuances: Chinese visitors want guides speaking their language, Indians are concerned about costs, Russians face language challenges, Americans want varied food options, Europeans seek public transport, and GCC visitors prefer human interaction.

Pillar 6: Memories

Performance: LOW

Emotionally charged and sensory-rich moments shape recall, with experiences outweighing destination narratives. No unifying story weakens emotional recall, lack of brand-led reinforcement after travel, unstructured memory sharing, and rarely collected feedback are key pain points.

Key Market Nuances: Chinese visitors need localized memory-sharing tools, Indians want structured ways to share memories, Russians need emotional follow-up, Americans want post-trip engagement, Europeans seek narrative closure, and GCC visitors expect brand reinforcement post-trip.

Strategic Recommendations

1. Develop a Unified Emotional Brand Narrative

High Priority

Abu Dhabi lacks a cohesive emotional imprint. Visitors remember individual attractions but struggle to describe what the destination stands for, limiting emotional depth and recommendation potential.

Key Actions:
  • Craft and embed a core emotional identity (e.g., "depth without overwhelm" or "modern serenity with soul") across all touchpoints
  • Amplify differentiation with Dubai by reinforcing unique positioning around cultural depth and relaxed sophistication
  • Build toolkits to align Yas Island, Etihad, cultural sites, and hospitality players under this unified tone and sensibility
  • Move from cultural display to cultural storytelling by adding human context and meaning to iconic sites
  • Develop a brand narrative workshop to create a unified emotional positioning that connects diverse offerings

2. Enhance Digital Discovery & Planning Experience

High Priority

Visitors face gaps in clarity, consistency, and trust when trying to move from inspiration to planning and booking. Official content lacks visibility on search engines and social platforms.

Key Actions:
  • Increase investment in platform-native content optimized for discovery algorithms (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Redbook)
  • Develop market-specific content strategies with stronger SEO presence to ensure visibility at the critical inspiration stage
  • Create a "One Abu Dhabi" unified visitor experience platform connecting airlines, hotels, attractions, and transport
  • Implement market-specific planning tools (e.g., Chinese-language hub with Alipay integration, Russian-language cultural guides)
  • Conduct a digital content audit to evaluate content visibility across key platforms

3. Implement Pre-Arrival & Post-Visit Engagement

Medium Priority

Abu Dhabi creates memorable experiences but fails to "claim" these memories through pre-arrival and post-trip engagement. Without structured follow-up, visitors' emotional connection fades and advocacy potential diminishes.

Key Actions:
  • Develop a pre-arrival communication strategy with personalized touchpoints based on visitor profiles
  • Create digital welcome kits, countdown content, and itinerary builders to maintain momentum between booking and arrival
  • Implement a post-visit engagement strategy with personalized thank-you messages and memory recaps
  • Develop market-specific memory activation tools (e.g., Redbook memory kit for Chinese visitors, WhatsApp sharing for Indians)
  • Create a journey mapping exercise to develop detailed communication plans for pre-arrival and post-visit phases

4. Address Market-Specific Pain Points

Medium Priority

Each source market experiences distinct friction points that require targeted solutions rather than one-size-fits-all approaches.

Key Actions:
  • China: Integrate with Chinese digital ecosystem (Alipay/WeChat Pay), provide Mandarin content and guides
  • India: Develop value-first family itineraries with bundled pricing and early booking discounts
  • Russia: Create Russian-language cultural planning journeys and payment workarounds
  • USA: Improve direct flight options and optimize official content for search visibility
  • Europe: Ensure accurate planning details and diverse accommodation options
  • GCC: Highlight privacy-focused accommodations and family-appropriate experiences

5. Leverage Local Residents as Travel Ambassadors

Low Priority

Visitors trust personal recommendations; locals can act as powerful ambassadors to strengthen decision confidence and amplify organic word-of-mouth.

Key Actions:
  • Develop a resident ambassador program to share authentic experiences and insider tips
  • Create resident-led storytelling content for social media and official platforms
  • Implement a referral incentive program for residents who bring visitors to Abu Dhabi
  • Integrate resident voices into cultural site experiences to add human context
  • Develop a "Voices of Abu Dhabi" digital layer across major landmarks with local stories

Visitor Journey Map

The research identified a comprehensive journey map that visualizes the entire visitor experience, highlighting key touchpoints, pain points, and moments of delight:

Key Journey Phases

  1. Inspiration & Decision Making - Where interest is sparked and destination is chosen
  2. Operations & Logistics - Planning flights, accommodation, and practical details
  3. Experiences & Activities Booking - Selecting and reserving specific activities
  4. Waiting Time & Excitement - The period between booking and arrival
  5. Actual Experiences - The in-destination experience from arrival to departure
  6. Memories - Post-trip reflection, sharing, and advocacy

Key Pain Points Across the Journey

  • Poor visibility of official content on search engines and social platforms
  • Limited direct flight options from USA and China
  • Limited or incorrect information about attractions
  • Lack of proactive communications post-booking
  • Transport challenges and currency exchange issues
  • No post-trip brand engagement or memory reactivation

Key Delight Moments

  • Strong visual presence on Instagram, YouTube, and TikTok
  • Visa-free access for key markets
  • Transparent pricing with secure payment options
  • Emotional anticipation driven by iconic experiences
  • Fast, intuitive airport experience with free SIM cards
  • Iconic attractions creating lasting emotional markers

Complaints & Feedback Insights

The research revealed important insights about how visitors handle complaints and provide feedback:

Key Contact Points for Issues

Visitors identified several entities as main points of contact for raising issues:

  • Security services, airport staff, and tourism specialists
  • The Ministry of Tourism and its affiliates are seen as vital credible sources
  • Hotel staff for accommodation-related concerns

Preferred Communication Channels

Different markets showed distinct preferences for complaint channels:

  • Chinese and Russian visitors prefer social media platforms
  • European and Indian visitors prefer email confirmation
  • GCC tourists have a higher preference for human interaction

Feedback Experience

Very few visitors reported major issues or complaints in Abu Dhabi. When feedback was provided, visitors were generally satisfied with the process and outcomes. Some visitors noted efforts to gather feedback through emoji-style touchscreens or post-travel surveys, which positively reflected on the Emirate's commitment to improvement.

Stakeholder-Specific Recommendations

The research identified specific actions for key stakeholders in the Abu Dhabi tourism ecosystem:

Etihad Airways

Role: First impression, emotional primer, and brand amplifier for Abu Dhabi

  • Launch "Your Abu Dhabi Countdown" — a dynamic pre-departure content stream tailored by market
  • Curate a "Meet Abu Dhabi" in-flight capsule with cultural storytelling and destination previews
  • Create a "Soft Landing Package" with in-flight pre-check-in for partner hotels
  • Send "Thank You for Flying Home to Abu Dhabi" messages post-visit with visual memory summaries

Abu Dhabi Government / Visit Abu Dhabi

Role: Narrative architect, ecosystem connector, and memory-maker

  • Launch a "One Abu Dhabi" unified visitor experience platform
  • Craft and embed a core emotional identity across all touchpoints
  • Roll out a "Your Journey Starts Here" interactive series with market-specific content
  • Curate a "Voices of Abu Dhabi" digital layer across major landmarks
  • Create a UGC Amplification Program featuring real traveler experiences

Zayed International Airport

Role: First physical touchpoint in Abu Dhabi's story

  • Enhance the free SIM card offering with Welcome Kits by Market
  • Create a dedicated ride-hailing zone with concierge-style assistance
  • Introduce "Welcome Corners" with dates, Arabic coffee, and cultural elements
  • Offer QR-based language onboarding at immigration/exit

Hospitality (Hotels, tours)

Role: Emotional anchor of the stay

  • Introduce market-specific welcome rituals tailored to cultural preferences
  • Equip staff with "Cultural Quick Cards" for market-specific interactions
  • Add in-room cultural cues that reflect Abu Dhabi's local identity
  • Offer "Cultural Touchpoint" evenings for longer-stay guests

Core Strategic Insight

"It was a collection of cool places, but I'm not sure what Abu Dhabi stands for."

This visitor quote encapsulates the central challenge identified in the research: Abu Dhabi delivers a frictionless trip but risks being remembered in parts, not as a powerful whole. The opportunity lies in building on the strong foundations of an overall seamless functional experience with a more confident, vocal, and emotively anchored proposition for Brand "Abu Dhabi".

Key Brand Challenges

  • Memories are being created but not owned by brand Abu Dhabi - Individual attractions promote themselves independently, but without synergy, there's no collective halo effect.
  • Culture is seen but not felt - Iconic landmarks are visually impressive but visitors often leave without deeper understanding or personal connection.
  • Digital discovery requires enhancement - As travelers shift to TikTok, Instagram, and AI tools for trip planning, Abu Dhabi must proactively own the discovery phase.

Conclusion

Abu Dhabi delivers a strong overall visitor experience with particular strengths in its airport efficiency, visa processes, hotel service excellence, and transportation accessibility. However, the research identifies significant opportunities to strengthen emotional connections, enhance digital discovery, improve pre-arrival engagement, and activate post-visit advocacy.

The most critical finding is that Abu Dhabi lacks a cohesive emotional imprint. Visitors remember individual attractions but struggle to describe what the destination stands for as a whole. This represents both a challenge and an opportunity to craft a more distinctive and emotionally resonant brand identity.

By implementing the strategic recommendations outlined in this report, Abu Dhabi can elevate its visitor experience from functionally excellent to emotionally memorable, strengthening its position as a premier global destination that creates lasting connections with visitors.
